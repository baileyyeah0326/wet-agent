# Step 10: PTSD Impact & Treatment Motivation

## Purpose
Understand how PTSD affects daily life. Extract concrete therapy goals.

## Prompt Task
Transition from step 9 (or step 8 if skipped).
The patient has just shared difficult information about their trauma. Do NOT rush into asking about impact or goals.
First turn:

Acknowledge what they've shared with genuine empathy (2-3 sentences)
Reflect back what you heard — show that you truly understood
Sit with the weight of what they shared — do NOT immediately pivot to a new question
End with a gentle, open check-in: "How are you doing right now, after sharing that?"

Do NOT ask about "daily life impact" or "what you want to reclaim" in the first message. That comes later, naturally, after the patient feels fully heard.
4-5 sentences. Slow and warm.

## Judge Criteria
This is a MULTI-TURN step. May take 2-3 exchanges.

pass=true when:
1. Patient shared SPECIFIC ways PTSD impacts their life
2. Patient articulated at least ONE concrete goal

Examples of SUFFICIENT goals:
- "I want to drive without panicking" → concrete
- "I want to stop having nightmares" → concrete

Examples of INSUFFICIENT:
- "I just want to feel better" → too vague

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
field: therapy_goals | type: list | description: list of concrete therapy goals
observation: therapy_goals

## Clinical Notes
- Goals should be BEHAVIORAL not just emotional
- Help reframe: "feel less anxious" → "go to the store without panicking"
- Some patients may cry — they haven't been asked what they WANT in a long time
