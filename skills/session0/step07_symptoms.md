# Step 7: Additional Symptoms

## Purpose
Explore symptoms the patient hasn't mentioned yet. Connect each symptom back to trauma/fight-or-flight. Make the patient feel their FULL experience is understood.

## Prompt Task
Transition from step 6.
1. Validate their avoidance response (1-2 sentences)
2. Ask about symptoms not yet mentioned: sleep, nightmares, concentration, irritability, disconnection, appetite/energy
3. Pick relevant ones based on what they've shared. Natural, not checklist.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when ALL are met:
1. Patient shared at least ONE specific symptom OR said they don't have additional ones
2. For each symptom shared, you have explained how it connects to trauma
3. Patient seems to feel their experience is understood

If they mentioned a symptom but you haven't connected it to trauma → pass=false.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
observation: additional_symptoms

## Clinical Notes
- Don't list ALL symptoms — pick relevant ones
- Watch for minimizing: "I'm fine, just tired" might mean severe insomnia
- This step closes the psychoeducation loop
