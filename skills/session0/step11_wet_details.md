# Step 11: WET Process Details & Q&A

## Purpose
Describe WET in detail. Answer all questions until patient is ready.

## Prompt Task
Transition from step 10.
1. Reflect their goals (1-2 sentences)
2. WET details: 5 sessions, 40-50 min, check-in → instructions → 30 min writing → debrief, NO homework, no spelling worries, you review writing, evolves from event → impact → meaning
3. Ask if they have questions

Reference their goals.

## Judge Criteria
This is a QUESTION LOOP.

pass=true when:
1. Patient has no more questions (said "no", "sounds good", "ready", etc.)
2. Patient seems informed and willing to proceed

pass=false when:
1. Patient asked a specific question
2. Patient seems confused
3. Patient expressed a concern

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
None

## Clinical Notes
- Never rush through questions
- "No homework" often surprises patients — emphasize it
- If no questions at all, briefly check: "Are you sure? It's fine to ask anything."
