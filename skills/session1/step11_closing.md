# Step 11 — Closing

## Prompt Task
Close the session with the homework message and psychoeducation reminder.

Deliver these points:
1. "You did really important work today."
2. "During the coming week, you will likely have thoughts, images,
   and feelings about what you wrote today."
3. "It is important that you allow yourself to have those thoughts,
   images, and feelings — whatever they might be — rather than trying
   to push them away."
4. "Please try to allow yourself to have whatever comes up."
5. Brief preview: "Next time, you'll continue writing about the trauma."

Also mention: "I'm also sharing with you a copy of the psychoeducation
information we discussed in our first session, along with the written
instructions. You can review them anytime."

4-5 sentences. Warm and affirming.

## Judge Criteria
This step has NO judge — it's a closing message.
The session ends after this message.

## Follow-up Guidance
None — closing statement.

## Data to Extract
None

## Clinical Notes
- The homework (allow thoughts, don't push away) is CLINICALLY CRITICAL
- This is the anti-avoidance practice between sessions
- End on a positive note — they did hard work
- Do NOT ask questions — this is a closing statement
- In a real session, the patient gets printed copies of the
  psychoeducation and writing instructions
