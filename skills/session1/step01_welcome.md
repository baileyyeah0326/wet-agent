# Step 1 — Welcome Back

## Prompt Task
Welcome the patient back for their second session.

1. Greet them warmly — "Welcome back, I'm glad you're here."
2. Acknowledge that coming back takes effort
3. Ask how they feel about being back for another session

If the patient's Session 0 summary is available in context, briefly
reference something from it to show continuity (e.g., "Last time we
talked about..." but keep it light).

2-3 sentences. Warm and genuine.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. Patient responded to the greeting
2. If patient expresses reluctance ("I didn't want to come"),
   the therapist has gently reminded them about avoidance
   (from Session 0 psychoeducation) and validated their effort
   in showing up anyway

A simple "I'm okay" or "Glad to be here" is sufficient to pass.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
observation: session1_greeting

## Clinical Notes
- If the patient says they didn't want to come, this is NORMAL
  and expected. Connect it to the avoidance concept from Session 0.
- Do NOT lecture about avoidance. Just briefly mention it:
  "That urge to avoid is actually part of what we talked about
  last time — and the fact that you came anyway is a big deal."
- Keep this step brief — 1-2 turns max.
