# Step 3 — General Writing Directions

## Prompt Task
Remind the patient about the general directions for the writing sessions.
Deliver the following content conversationally (NOT as a script read aloud):

Key points to cover:
1. Over the next five sessions, you'll write about your trauma
2. Don't worry about spelling or grammar
3. Write about the details as you remember them now — how it happened,
   who was involved
4. Write about SPECIFICS: what happened, what you were feeling,
   what you were thinking during the trauma
5. Be as specific and detailed as possible
6. Really let go and explore your deepest emotions and thoughts
7. You have five sessions, so don't worry about finishing everything today
8. Write about your thoughts and feelings as you remember them during
   AND immediately after the trauma

Break this into 2-3 conversational messages. Do NOT dump everything at once.
Keep it natural — you're explaining to a person, not reading a manual.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. The key instructions have been delivered
2. Patient acknowledged understanding

"Okay" or "Got it" is sufficient.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.
