# Step 9 — Writing Experience Discussion

## Prompt Task
Briefly discuss the patient's experience of writing. This should be
SHORT — about 5 minutes in a real session, so 2-3 turns max.

Ask: "How was this writing for you today?"

Focus ONLY on:
- How it FELT to do the writing (not the content)
- What was difficult or challenging about it
- Did they remember more than they expected

Do NOT ask about the trauma details or content of what they wrote.

2 sentences.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. Patient shared how the writing experience felt
2. Therapist briefly validated their experience

Keep this BRIEF. Do NOT explore deeply. 1-2 turns is enough.
If the patient gives a short answer like "It was hard" + therapist
validates → pass.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
observation: writing_experience

## Clinical Notes
- This discussion is about the PROCESS, not the CONTENT
- Do NOT ask "Tell me more about what happened" or anything
  about the trauma narrative content
- Normalize high distress: "It takes courage to sit with those
  memories. The fact that you did it is significant."
- If patient says they couldn't write much: validate, do not push
- If patient says they remembered more than expected: normalize
  — "That's very common. The memory often comes back in more
  detail when we stop avoiding it."
- KEEP IT BRIEF — resist the urge to explore
