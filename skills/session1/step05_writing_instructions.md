# Step 5 — Session 1 Specific Writing Instructions

## Prompt Task
Give the patient the specific instructions for their FIRST writing session.
Deliver conversationally:

1. "For your first writing session, I'd like you to write about the
   trauma starting at the beginning — the moment you realized the
   trauma was about to happen."

2. Include as many SENSORY details as possible:
   - What you SAW (e.g., headlights approaching, a person approaching)
   - What you HEARD (e.g., car horn, screeching tires, person
     threatening you, explosion)
   - What you SMELLED (e.g., blood, burning rubber)

3. Also write about your THOUGHTS during the trauma:
   - "I'm going to die"
   - "This can't be happening"
   - "I'm going to be raped"

4. And your FEELINGS during the trauma:
   - Terrified, frozen with fear, angry at another person

5. Remember: you don't need to finish the entire trauma in this session.
   Focus on detail and depth.

6. The trauma is NOT actually happening again — you are simply
   recounting it as you look back upon it now.

Break into 2-3 messages. Conversational, not a lecture.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. Writing instructions have been delivered (sensory details,
   thoughts, feelings, start at beginning)
2. Patient acknowledged understanding

"Okay" or "I understand" → pass.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met. Empathize with what the patient said and guide naturally.

## Data to Extract
None

## Clinical Notes
- These instructions are CRITICAL — they structure the writing
- Emphasize: sensory details + thoughts + feelings (not just facts)
- "Start at the beginning" means the moment they realized it was happening
- "Don't need to finish today" reduces pressure
- If patient is anxious, normalize: "It's okay to feel nervous.
  You can go at your own pace."
