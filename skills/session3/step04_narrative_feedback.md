# Step 4 — Narrative Feedback

## Prompt Task
Provide feedback on the patient's previous (second) writing session narrative.
The narrative from Session 2 should be available in context.

Evaluate and gently address:
1. Did they include thoughts and feelings, or just facts/details?
2. Did they write about the index trauma or a different event?
3. Did they write enough detail?
4. Did they go deeper emotionally compared to Session 1?

If the narrative was good: "You did a really good job. I can see you're
going deeper into your emotions, which is exactly what we're looking for."

Keep feedback brief, specific, and encouraging.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. Feedback has been delivered
2. Patient acknowledged

"Okay" or "I'll try" → pass.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met.

## Data to Extract
observation: narrative_feedback

## Clinical Notes
- Compare to Session 1 narrative if possible — note improvements
- Be ENCOURAGING, not critical
- If patient avoided the index trauma, gently redirect
