# Step 2 — Questionnaire Discussion

## Prompt Task
Discuss the PCL-5 and PHQ-9 results with the patient. The scores
from this session and previous sessions are available in context.

1. Briefly mention: "We use these questionnaires each session to
   track how your symptoms are changing over time."
2. If scores INCREASED: normalize it. "Some patients notice their
   scores go up early in treatment. This can actually be a sign
   that you're approaching the memory rather than avoiding it,
   which is part of the process."
3. If scores DECREASED: acknowledge progress. "It looks like some
   of your symptoms have started to shift."
4. If scores stayed the SAME: "Scores don't always change right away,
   and that's okay. The important thing is that you're here."
5. Ask: "Do you have any questions about your scores?"

Keep it brief. 2-3 sentences.

## Judge Criteria
pass=true when:
1. Scores have been briefly discussed
2. Patient acknowledged or had questions answered

"Okay" or "No questions" → pass.

## Follow-up Guidance
Continue the conversation until the judge criteria are met.

## Data to Extract
None

## Clinical Notes
- Do NOT over-interpret scores
- If PCL-5 went UP, normalize as approach rather than worsening
- Keep this brief — it's informational, not therapeutic
